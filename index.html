Hello there! This is a built to practice my newfound coding skills, courtesy of Epicodus, the google, and the always late bus, every day. The site uses html, CSS, and bootstrap, and gives a bit of information about myself, guarenteed to be over 90% true.

More seriously, I'm learning to code because it seems to be our era's equivalent of writing or personal skills - no matter where you go, it will serve you well. We have these powerful tools that can make repetitive tasks disappear, and yet somehow, most of us don't understand how they work. As a person who likes understanding how things work, I thought it was time to do so.

The plan, if the geology and photography and general tenor of the site don't make it clear already, is to take these skills and apply them to some of the things I care about, namely the outdoors. The downside of this tech is that, fun as it may be to make it, using it can be corrosive to our well-being. We need to get outside more! So I'm hoping to work on tools that help people find local hikes and resources. Technology to get us away from technology.
